The Challenge

A talent surplus.
A system deficit.

Nyamirambo has more creative talent than most cities twice its size. What it lacks is the infrastructure to turn that talent into income. CreativeLink was built to close that gap.

0

Jobs Rwanda's NST2 failed to create between 2017 and 2023 — felt most acutely in communities like Nyamirambo.

The Problem

What we found in Nyamirambo

Five community interviews and deep qualitative research uncovered five compounding barriers that keep creative youth from earning.

15.4%

Youth Unemployment

Nyamirambo's youth unemployment sits well above the national average — concentrated among 18–35 year-olds with creative skills but no formal earning pathway.

🔧

Equipment Barrier

Tailors and photographers routinely borrow equipment — eliminating most of their profit margin. Owning tools is the single biggest income unlock, but it requires upfront capital nobody has.

📢

No Marketing System

Creative youth have no way to attract clients beyond their immediate street. Word-of-mouth is the only channel — invisible to anyone outside the neighborhood.

📱

Digital Skills Gap

4 out of 5 youth interviewed own smartphones but none use them commercially. The device is there. The knowledge to monetize it is not.

🌐

English-Only Platforms

LinkedIn, Upwork, and most learning platforms are English-only — excluding the Kinyarwanda and French-speaking majority of creative youth in Rwanda.

🤝

No Mentor Access

Creative professionals exist in Kigali, but there is no system to connect them with youth in Nyamirambo. Mentorship happens by accident, not by design.

Root Cause Analysis

The 5 Whys

We traced the surface problem — youth unemployment — back to its structural root using the 5 Whys framework.

W1

Why are youth unemployed?

They cannot monetize their creative skills despite having them.

W2

Why can't they monetize skills?

They have no market access, no client network, and no visibility beyond their block.

W3

Why no market access?

No platform exists that aggregates creative talent in Nyamirambo for clients to discover.

W4

Why no such platform?

Existing platforms are English-only, desktop-first, and not built for informal creative economies.

ROOT

Root Cause

Limited recognition and integration of the creative sector into local job creation and investment initiatives — leaving talented youth without a functional system to turn skills into income.
